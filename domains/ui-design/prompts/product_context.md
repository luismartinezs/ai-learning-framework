[PRODUCT CONTEXT]
Menu Decoder is used by travelers with dietary restrictions —
people who are anxious, sitting in an unfamiliar restaurant abroad,
often at a table with others waiting. The person using this has a
real health risk if they eat the wrong thing.

The UI must feel calm and trustworthy, not clinical or alarming.
Safety status is the single most important piece of information
on the screen — it should be immediately scannable without reading.
The color system (green/amber/red) carries all the critical meaning
and should dominate the visual hierarchy.

The user is relieved when something is safe. They are anxious when
something is flagged. The design should support both emotional states
without feeling like a medical alert system.
