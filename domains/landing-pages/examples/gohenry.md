## Copy Sections

### Headline
Not quoted verbatim. The winning variant addressed the parent's immediate question: "What is this, and is it safe for my child?" The page opened by positioning Visa prominently, even to the extent that the GoHenry brand was marginalized.

### Subheadline
Not documented separately. The page was structured to handle impulsive, low-attention mobile visitors.

### Hero Body
Not documented verbatim. The winning mobile landing page was "2.5 meters tall on mobile." It systematically addressed every parental objection in sequence, starting with the strongest.

Multiple CTAs were sprinkled throughout the page so visitors could convert at any point once convinced. "This effectively made the page as short as possible, but as long as necessary."

### Primary CTA
Not quoted verbatim. Multiple CTAs throughout the long page, each presented after a key objection was addressed.

### CTA Context
Free trial was the primary counter-objection on the pricing page. Parents compared GoHenry to bank accounts (which are free), so the copy communicated that GoHenry is much more than a payment account: it's an online learning tool.

### Key Benefits
Not presented as a traditional feature list. The approach was visual objection-handling rather than feature-listing:

- Visual diagrams showing how the product works (complex information presented visually for mobile attention spans)
- Images showing how much "stuff" you get with the subscription
- Section covering all free features bundled with GoHenry
- Positioned as financial education tool, not just a debit card

### Social Proof
Visa branding positioned as the dominant trust signal, more prominent than GoHenry's own brand. "By positioning Visa much more prominently as part of the proposition, even to the extent that the GoHenry brand was marginalized, we leveraged the trust inherent in Visa and made it our own."

This was the answer to a seemingly impossible challenge: selling a new financial product for children (combining two of the toughest trust problems in marketing).

### Secondary Sections

**Three key differences between mobile and desktop visitors:**
"Don't assume that your mobile visitors are just desktop visitors on a different device."

1. **Mobile users were more impulsive.** Many landed on the site on a whim from social media, knowing much less about what was being offered.
2. **50% less likely to have their children with them when they signed up.** Since GoHenry is for children, this affected both the funnel mechanics and the messaging.
3. **40% more likely to have concerns about trust and security.** Mobile felt less secure for entering financial details for a child.

**Facebook ad optimization (191% increase):**
Applied multivariate testing to ad creatives, testing over 100 Facebook ads. Combined optimized ads with winning landing pages. Every interaction a prospect has with the business is an optimization opportunity, not just the website.

**Pricing page optimization (36% increase):**
Addressed three specific objections:
1. Price pain: parents compared to free bank accounts. Counter: GoHenry is an online learning tool, not just a payment account.
2. Lock-in fears: concerns about being trapped in a subscription. Counter: free trial as primary counter-objection.
3. Value communication: added section covering all free features bundled with GoHenry plus visual showing how much "stuff" you get.

"Many companies make the mistake of assuming that they don't need to do any selling on their pricing page."

**Homepage optimization (23% increase):**
The winning landing page also beat the existing homepage. Research showed half of homepage visitors originally came from the same source as landing page visitors (returning for a second look). They had the same objections and were persuaded by the same appeals. "If you have winning content, look for other places you can use it."

**Offline optimization (11% card activation increase):**
Research revealed half of children knew about the card before it arrived. Parents were more likely to activate when their child knew. Solution: sent the welcome letter to the kids instead of the parents. Card activations increased by 11%.

**Card activation flow (8% uplift):**
Usability studies revealed: (1) activation began with standard member login but many parents didn't consider themselves "members" yet, so they weren't sure they were in the right place; (2) several "cognitive barriers" diverted attention (unnecessary decisions about weekly allowance amount and weeks to load). Removed both unnecessary decisions. "Be ruthless. Focus only on what's necessary to complete the task at hand."

**Key principles:**
1. Mobile visitors are a separate audience with different objections, not just desktop visitors on a smaller screen.
2. Look for "hidden wealth" in your business: persuasion assets (like the Visa partnership) that prospects rarely see.
3. "There's no such thing as a too-long page, only a too-boring one."
4. Optimize every interaction, not just the website. Facebook ads, offline mailings, and activation flows are all optimization targets.
