### Hero Body
The case study winning variant was approximately 20x longer than the original minimal homepage. It systematically addressed four visitor objections identified through research (detailed below in Secondary Sections).

### Primary CTA
"Continue" (with email input field)

### CTA Context
"Start your 30-day FREE trial. Cancel anytime."

On the checkout page, visitors objected to entering a credit card for a free trial. The winning checkout added an explanation box right under the free-trial button explaining: Crazy Egg would not charge anything during the free trial, and having a card on file prevents any one person signing up for multiple free trials.

Visitors were also confused by subscription rates quoted in both monthly and yearly amounts. The winning version clarified how the card would be charged for the yearly rate only after the free trial, with cancel/downgrade available at any time.

### Secondary Sections

**The four objections that drove the 30% homepage increase:**

1. **"What exactly are heatmaps?"** -- Visitors were unclear about the reports Crazy Egg would generate. Solution: customer case-study competition. Used actual customers' language to explain heatmaps, not marketing speak.

2. **"Is it worth the price?"** -- Prospects couldn't justify the cost. Solution: reframing via academic research. Carnegie Mellon University research showed "88 percent correlation between eye movement and mouse movement." Formal eye-tracking studies cost six figures and take months. Crazy Egg delivers comparable visitor intelligence at a tiny fraction of the cost and time.

3. **"How is this different from Google Analytics?"** -- Visitors thought Crazy Egg was just an overlay report. Solution: incorporated transcripts from Crazy Egg's customer support team explaining the differences (they were experienced at this conversation).

4. **"Doesn't it have fewer features than competitors?"** -- Solution: reframed. Fewer features means less data overload, not less insight. "Sometimes less is more."

**Explainer video (64% increase):**
Three-minute script encapsulating key sales points. Produced by Demo Duck. Hosted on Wistia. Even though the video's message was similar to the page text, the video version generated 64% more conversions. Different learning styles: some people prefer watching over reading.

**One-time offer (25% acceptance, significant revenue lift):**
Offered a discount for longer-term commitment to customers who just signed up. Shown once to each customer. Key copy element: explained the reason behind the discount, and changed a "yes/no" decision into a "maybe" decision to reduce perceived risk. Even after factoring in lost revenue from customers who would have paid month-to-month, the offer considerably increased annual revenue.

**Checkout optimization (116% increase):**
Used Qualaroo on-page surveys at the exact checkout step to discover what was on visitors' minds right there. Found two main objections:
1. Credit card for free trial felt like a scam
2. Monthly vs. yearly pricing was confusing

Added explanation boxes addressing both. "A confused mind hesitates, and hesitation is the death of a sale."

**Key principles:**
1. "You cannot have a page that's too long, only one that's too boring."
2. Test different media for the same persuasive content.
3. Use targeted on-page surveys (Qualaroo) on individual pages to discover page-specific objections.
4. "Don't let the fear of a short-term loss stand in the way of a long-term gain."
