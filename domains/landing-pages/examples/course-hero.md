## Copy Sections

### Headline
Not documented as a single hero headline. The winning variant led with community scale as the primary hook on the account creation page.

### Subheadline
"More than 10 million students and educators" (community size proof as lead positioning)

### Hero Body
Not documented verbatim. The winning variant emphasized platform scale and resource breadth before asking for signup. Key shift: addressed four specific visitor objections identified through research.

### Primary CTA
"Create my FREE account"

Repositioned above the Facebook signup button. Research showed Facebook signup was creating friction (students didn't want to connect Facebook to a study tool). The standard email signup was elevated as the default path.

### CTA Context
"Free to create an account" was made prominent and repeated throughout subheadings and the CTA itself. Cost uncertainty was a key objection: visitors weren't sure if creating an account cost anything.

### Key Benefits
Copy elements added to the winning account creation page:
- "More than 10 million students and educators" (credibility/scale)
- "Hundreds of expert tutors" (quality proof)
- "The largest (and best) collection" of study resources (positioning)
- "Thousands of flashcards" (resource depth)
- Rewritten bullet points describing what users get (original bullets were unclear about value)

### Social Proof
Community size ("more than 10 million students and educators") was the primary trust mechanism. Scale implied both legitimacy and resource depth.

### Secondary Sections

**Four objections on the account creation page (19% increase in paid members):**

1. **Lack of awareness of credibility and size.** Visitors didn't trust Course Hero. Counter: mentioned community scale ("more than 10 million students and educators," "hundreds of expert tutors") and resource claims ("the largest (and best) collection," "thousands of flashcards").

2. **Confusion about how Course Hero works.** Counter: rewrote bullet points to describe what users actually get, in clearer language.

3. **Uncertainty about cost.** It's free to create an account, but visitors didn't know that. Counter: made "free" prominent, repeated it in subheadings and the CTA ("Create my FREE account").

4. **Concerns about Facebook signup.** Counter: de-emphasized "Sign Up with Facebook" by moving it below the standard signup button. "If your website has multiple ways to sign up, allow your visitors to choose which option they prefer."

**Upload page optimization (21% uploads, 24% cash deposits):**
The original page showed both free upload and paid access options side by side. This created two competing conversations.

Solution: removed all mention of the free upload option from the main view and focused messaging exclusively on paid access. Created an overlay that effectively down-sold users on the upload option if they wanted it.

"You might expect that by promoting the free option we'd have lowered revenues. But by separating the goals, the number of cash deposits rose too, by 24%."

This is the "one conversation at a time" principle for two-sided platforms. Don't show right content to wrong visitors. Show the right content, with the right CTA, to the right visitors, at the right time in their journey.

**Study resources sidebar (9% increase in paid members):**
Web analytics revealed study resource pages were among the top landing pages (visitors came from Google to specific study materials). Surveys on these pages showed visitors had unanswered questions about how Course Hero works.

Solution: added a sidebar explaining what Course Hero is and why it's valuable. Previous A/B tests had proven the sidebar was "a nerve that was sensitive to conversion" so they knew changes there would have impact.

**Platform strategy context:**
- "For many types of online businesses, the winner takes all." Platforms, marketplaces, and communities especially.
- Speed matters: (i) learn and iterate fast, (ii) improve conversion rate, (iii) reinvest additional profits to acquire more traffic.
- "There's an enormous advantage to being in the lead rather than lagging behind."

**Key principles:**
1. Two-sided platforms must optimize both sides, but have one conversation at a time per page.
2. Each page context needs its own copy strategy (account creation visitors need credibility; upload visitors need a clear default action; search landing visitors need product education).
3. De-emphasize lower-converting paths without removing them (overlay vs. inline).
4. Focus on evidence-based experiments with research-driven hypotheses, not guessing.
