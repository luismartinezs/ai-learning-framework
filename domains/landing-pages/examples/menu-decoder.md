### Hero Body
You have a peanut allergy and you are sitting in a restaurant in Thailand. The menu is in Thai. The waiter does not speak English. Google Translate gives you word-by-word gibberish but tells you nothing about the shrimp paste hiding in the curry.

Menu Decoder reads the entire menu from your photo, translates every dish, and flags anything that conflicts with your dietary profile. Red means avoid. Yellow means ask. Green means likely OK.

### Secondary Sections
Objection handling:

"But I can just use Google Translate." Google Translate gives you literal word translations. It does not know that Pad Thai traditionally contains shrimp paste, or that the green curry uses fish sauce. Menu Decoder analyzes the cuisine, not just the words.

"What if it gets it wrong?" Menu Decoder is a translation tool, not a medical guarantee. It flags risks you would otherwise miss entirely. The Staff Card feature lets you verify with the restaurant directly.

"I'm only traveling for a week." The Trip Pass costs less than a single hospital visit abroad. Less than one ruined dinner. $4.99 for 14 days of confidence at every meal.
