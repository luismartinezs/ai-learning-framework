## Product Description
What the product is and what it does. 2-4 sentences.
Be specific about the mechanism — HOW it works, not just WHAT it does.

## Target Audience
Who uses this product. Be specific:
- Demographics or role
- The specific situation or context they are in when they need this
- What they already know and believe when they arrive

## The Moment of Need
Describe the exact moment, situation, or frustration that makes
someone seek this product. Be concrete — describe the scene, not
an abstraction.

## Primary Problem Solved
The specific pain, fear, or desire the product addresses.
One sentence. Not a feature — an outcome or relief.

## Key Mechanism
The specific thing this product does that makes the promise
believable. One sentence a non-expert would understand.
If this product has academic research, specific data, or a
unique technical approach that differentiates it — include it here.

## Trust Barriers
What makes a visitor skeptical or hesitant? List the 2-3 most
likely reasons a qualified visitor would not convert.
Be honest — include price concerns, "too good to be true" reactions,
safety concerns, or "I could do this without paying" objections.

## Real Data
Any specific numbers, statistics, or proof points available:
- Customer counts or usage numbers
- Performance metrics (accuracy rates, time to result, etc.)
- Pricing (exact, not ranges)
- Specific customer results or savings (real examples if available)
- Awards, certifications, or institutional backing

## Primary Conversion Goal
What is the single action you want the visitor to take?
(e.g. "start free trial", "book a demo", "download the app")

## CTA Commitment Level
How much commitment does your primary CTA ask for?
[low = free/no card | medium = card required/free trial | high = paid/demo request]
