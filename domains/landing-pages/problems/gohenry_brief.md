## Product Description

GoHenry is a fintech product that provides children aged 8-18 with a Visa debit card linked to a parent-controlled account. Parents set spending limits, allowances, and savings goals through a web or mobile app. Children use the physical Visa card for purchases. The product is designed as a financial education tool, not just a payment method. It teaches children money management through real transactions with parental oversight. The company is ranked in the FinTech 100.

## Target Audience

Parents of children aged 8-18. They arrive primarily from social media (Facebook) ads, often on mobile, browsing impulsively without their child present. They have little or no prior knowledge of the product. They are protective of their children and skeptical of unfamiliar financial products targeting minors. Mobile visitors are significantly more concerned about trust and security than desktop visitors.

## Primary Problem Solved

Parents want to teach their children financial responsibility but lack practical tools to do so. Giving cash provides no tracking or structure. Adding a child to an existing bank account offers no parental controls or educational scaffolding. The gap is between wanting kids to learn money skills and having a safe, controlled environment for them to practice with real money.

## Key Mechanism

Pairs a child-facing Visa debit card (real transactions, real consequences) with a parent-facing control layer (spending limits, allowance automation, activity monitoring). The Visa network provides universal merchant acceptance and institutional trust. The parent app provides the safety net and visibility. Children learn by doing, with guardrails.

## Constraints

- Monthly subscription model (competes against free bank accounts in parent perception)
- Cold traffic: most visitors arrive from social media ads with zero product awareness
- Two trust barriers: (1) it's a financial product, and (2) it's for children. Both independently difficult.
- Primarily mobile audience (different behavior patterns than desktop)
- Parents often sign up without their child present, which affects the onboarding flow
- Card requires physical delivery and activation, adding friction between signup and value realization
- Partnership with Visa is a major trust asset but wasn't historically emphasized
- Ages 8-18 target range
