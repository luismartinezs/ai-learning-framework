## Product Description

Crazy Egg is a website analytics tool focused on visual behavior data. It generates heatmaps showing where visitors click, scroll maps showing how far they read, session recordings of individual visitor journeys, and overlay reports mapping clicks to page elements. The platform also includes A/B testing, on-site surveys, and popup/sticky bar deployment. Setup involves adding a JavaScript snippet to the target site. The tool runs across unlimited domains with unlimited team seats.

## Target Audience

Website owners and digital marketers who have traffic but don't understand why it isn't converting. Ranges from small business owners running their own sites to enterprise marketing teams optimizing high-traffic pages. They typically already use Google Analytics for traffic numbers but lack visibility into what visitors actually do on each page. They arrive already aware that their site has a conversion problem.

## Primary Problem Solved

Website owners can see traffic volumes and bounce rates in traditional analytics, but they can't see why visitors leave or where they get stuck. The gap between "people visit my site" and "I understand what they experience" is invisible with standard analytics. Traditional eye-tracking studies that answer these questions cost six figures and take months.

## Key Mechanism

Tracks mouse movement and click behavior as a proxy for visual attention (academic research shows 88% correlation between eye movement and mouse movement), then renders this data as visual heatmaps and recordings. This gives website owners eye-tracking-grade behavioral insight without the cost or logistics of formal eye-tracking studies. The visual format makes the data immediately actionable without analytics expertise.

## Constraints

- Free trial model (30-day trial, credit card required upfront)
- Tiered subscription pricing (monthly and yearly options)
- Requires JavaScript snippet installation on target site
- Competes directly against Google Analytics (free) in prospect perception, though the actual functionality is different
- Simpler feature set than some competitors, which is both a positioning challenge and an advantage (less data overload)
